Key Government Insights From Page 3

#1 ONGC is the backbone at 61.49% — India's national oil company produces nearly two thirds of all domestic crude. This means India's energy security is heavily concentrated in one public sector entity — a significant policy risk if ONGC faces operational challenges.

#2 OIL contributes 20.18% — Oil India Limited is the second largest contributor. Combined with ONGC, the two PSUs account for over 80% of all indigenous production — showing private sector participation remains very limited.

#3 JVC/Private sector at only 11.61% — Despite decades of liberalization policies, private and joint venture companies contribute barely 1 in 9 barrels of domestic crude. A clear signal that India's upstream oil sector needs more private investment incentives.

#4 Stacked area chart tells a stable story — Production stays consistently between 2K–3K (000 MT) across all 4 years with no dramatic growth. India's domestic crude output is essentially flat — meaning ALL growth in refinery throughput (Page 1 showed ~14% growth) is being met by increased imports, not domestic production.

#5 RIL Jamnagar dominates refining at 69K — Reliance Industries' Jamnagar refinery is India's largest by a huge margin — processing nearly twice what the next largest refinery handles. It's also the world's largest refining complex. The SEZ unit adds another 65K making RIL alone responsible for nearly 50% of all crude processed in India.

#6 21 active refineries across India — A strong infrastructure base but heavily concentrated in Gujarat (RIL), Karnataka (MRPL), Kerala (BPCL-Kochi), and Odisha (IOCL-Paradip).
